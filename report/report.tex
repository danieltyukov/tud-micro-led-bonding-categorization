\begin{abstract}
This work presents a 93~mm $\times$ 93~mm two-layer FR-4 PCB designed to
characterize how different solder pastes perform when bonding micro-LEDs.
The board carries 26 Würth WL-SFCC 0404 RGB LEDs (8 addressable, 18 in two
daisy chains), four 0402 NTCs for forward-voltage thermometry, a 100~$\Omega$
0.1\% reference resistor with OPEN/SHORT/LOAD pads for impedance
spectroscopy, and a set of TLM ladders and van der Pauw cloverleaves on the
same substrate. Putting all of this on one PCB means a single cleanroom
session yields bond resistance, contact resistivity, sheet resistance, and
junction-to-substrate thermal resistance from the same hardware. No
separate test coupons, no inter-board correlation. Two 32-pin headers break
every active net out to a breadboard. The board is fab-ready for the
Aisler or Eurocircuits standard pool with ENIG plating; LEDs are
deliberately left unassembled,
since the bond joint itself is the research subject and has to form under
controlled cleanroom conditions. The report covers the schematic, the
characterization methods, the tools needed to run them, and the four-paste
comparison the design was built for.
\end{abstract}

\section{Introduction}

Mounting a sub-millimeter LED die on a PCB sounds simple until you measure
it. The joint has to carry current with a low voltage drop, pull heat out
of the die fast enough to keep it from cooking itself, and survive whatever
thermomechanical cycling the application throws at it. With SAC305 reflow
paste, the cheap default, the quality of that joint depends on paste
rheology, stencil aperture, reflow profile, and substrate finish.
Alternative chemistries (Au-Sn eutectic \cite{yin2024ausn}, Cu-Cu direct
bonding \cite{wang2023cucu}, polymer-reinforced pastes
\cite{ji2023polymer}) trade one of those parameters for another.

This board extends the v1 design Abdelwahab et al.\ shipped at ECTC 2025
\cite{abdelwahab2025ectc}. The v1 measured bond yield and V$_F$ only. v2
adds three more characterization methods (TLM contact resistivity, van der
Pauw sheet resistance, EIS impedance) on the same substrate, so one
cleanroom session produces a complete electrical fingerprint of whatever
paste-and-reflow combination was run. The design deliberately targets the
Aisler / Eurocircuits standard pool, not a semiconductor fab, to keep the
boards cheap (\textasciitilde\euro40--50 each) and reproducible at any
university lab.

  \label{fig:board}

The board (Fig.~\ref{fig:board}) is organized top to bottom into five
strips. The top edge has the title block and the north 32-pin header. Of
the 32 pins, 8 are pre-routed to the chain endpoints and the NTC sense
lines; the other 24 are bare for jumpering, which is useful when bypassing
the breadboard to probe a test structure directly. Below the header sits
the 6$\times$6 DoE bond-pad array at 3.5~mm pitch, with the EIS
calibration block in the same horizontal band: bare OPEN/SHORT pads and a
100~$\Omega$ 0.1\% reference resistor that doubles as the LOAD reference.
The middle strip is the test-structure zone: three TLM ladders at
$W \in \{0.25, 0.5, 1.0\}$~mm with gaps of 200, 300, 500, 1000, 2000, and
4000~$\mu$m, plus four VDP cloverleaves at $W \in \{1.0, 0.5, 0.25, 0.15\}$~mm.
The smallest TLM gap stays above the standard-pool minimum at both Aisler
and Eurocircuits, so the ladders fab in the same run, on the same ENIG
surface, as the LED-side traces. The bottom strip carries the eight WL-SFCC LEDs (D1--D8) with the
four NTCs interleaved between them, the DCL6 and DCL12 daisy chains
(wired through the K$_R$ cathode), and the 32-pin south header that
pre-routes every D1--D8 signal out to a ribbon cable.

Design rule budget: 0.20~mm minimum copper track, 0.30~mm minimum copper
clearance (1.3$\times$ and 2$\times$ over the 0.15~mm standard-pool minimum
at both Aisler and Eurocircuits), 0.30~mm minimum drill, 0.15~mm via
annular ring. DRC returns zero violations and zero unconnected items. ENIG
plating (Ni 4~$\mu$m, Au 0.075~$\mu$m) is non-negotiable: every probe pad
has to be gold for reliable probe contact, and the customer-bonded LEDs
need a gold surface to wet against. Component count: 192 footprints in
total, 69 of them assembled by the fab (4 NTCs, the reference resistor, 64
header pins) and 123 left for the customer (the 26 LEDs bonded in the
cleanroom and 97 bare-ENIG test lands).

The companion KiCad schematic captures the 36 electrically active
components (the 26 LEDs, 4 NTCs, the resistor, 3 EIS test points, and the
two connectors) and the 164 named nets. The remaining 123 footprints are
isolated single-pad test structures; they appear on the PCB and in the BOM
but adding them to the schematic would clutter it without information, so
they are documented through the on-board silk and the project README
instead.

\section{Characterization Methods}

\subsection{Forward-voltage thermometry (V$_F$-TSP)}

A diode's forward voltage at a fixed, low sense current falls roughly
linearly with junction temperature over a wide range
\cite{pangallo2018direct}. The slope, $S \equiv -\partial V_F / \partial
T_J$, is constant enough to invert for $T_J$ in real time. For a sense
current $I_S \ll I_{op}$,
\begin{equation}
  T_J(t) \;=\; T_{J,0} \;-\; \frac{1}{S}\bigl[V_F(t) - V_{F,0}\bigr],
  \label{eq:vftsp}
\end{equation}
where $V_{F,0}$ is the forward voltage at the calibration temperature
$T_{J,0}$. The LED $V_F$ is read across the four bonded contacts of the
device under test via the south header. The substrate reference comes from the four
NTCs (TH1--TH4, Murata NCP15XH103J03RC, $R_{25}=10$~k$\Omega$, $B_{25/85}=3380$~K
\cite{murata_ncp15}) interleaved between the LED pairs and read through
the north header. The Steinhart-B equation maps NTC resistance to
temperature:
\begin{equation}
  \frac{1}{T} \;=\; \frac{1}{T_0} \;+\; \frac{1}{B}\,\ln\!\left(\frac{R}{R_0}\right),
  \label{eq:ntc}
\end{equation}
with $T_0 = 298.15$~K, $R_0 = 10$~k$\Omega$. To avoid self-heating bias
the sense current is pulsed (100~$\mu$A, 1~ms, 10\% duty), following the
pulsed differential V/I technique of Roscam Abbing and Pertijs
\cite{roscamabbing2011led}, which also has the advantage of requiring
calibration at only a single temperature. The bond-joint thermal resistance is then the
steady-state ratio of the junction-to-substrate temperature difference to
the dissipated power:
\begin{equation}
  R_{th,bond} \;=\; \lim_{t \to \infty}\frac{T_J(t) - T_{NTC}(t)}{P_{op}}.
\end{equation}

\subsection{Electrochemical impedance spectroscopy (EIS)}

EIS sweeps a 10~mV AC excitation from 10$^{-1}$~Hz to 10$^{6}$~Hz and
records the complex impedance $Z(j\omega) = Z' + jZ''$. The Nyquist plot
$Z''(Z')$ separates the series bond resistance (the high-frequency
intercept, $Z' \rightarrow R_s$ as $\omega \rightarrow \infty$) from the
diode's parallel RC dynamics (the low-frequency semicircle). For the
standard LED equivalent circuit ($R_s$ in series with $R_p \parallel C_j$),
\begin{equation}
  Z(j\omega) \;=\; R_s \;+\; \frac{R_p}{1 + j\omega R_p C_j}.
  \label{eq:eis}
\end{equation}
EIS is only as good as its calibration. The board carries three on-PCB
references: \texttt{EIS\_OPEN} (two isolated 1.27~mm gold pads,
$Z \rightarrow \infty$), \texttt{EIS\_SHORT} (paired pads joined by a
solder bridge, $Z \rightarrow 0$), and \texttt{EIS\_LOAD} (the 100~$\Omega$
0.1\% reference resistor, TCR 25~ppm/K \cite{vishay_tnpw}). The resistor
pads are oversized so they double as four-wire Kelvin probe lands.

\subsection{Transmission line model (TLM)}

TLM gives sheet resistance $R_{sh}$ and specific contact resistivity
$\rho_c$ from one set of resistance measurements between successive
finger pairs at known gaps $d$. For $N$ identical contacts of width $W$
and characteristic transfer length $L_T$,
\begin{equation}
  R_{tot}(d) \;=\; \frac{R_{sh}}{W}\,d \;+\; 2\,R_c,
  \quad
  R_c \;=\; \frac{R_{sh}\,L_T}{W}\coth\!\left(\frac{L}{L_T}\right),
  \label{eq:tlm}
\end{equation}
where $L$ is the contact length. A linear fit of $R_{tot}$ vs $d$ gives
$R_{sh}$ from the slope and $2 R_c$ from the intercept; $\rho_c$ then
follows from $\rho_c = R_c \cdot W \cdot L_T \cdot \tanh(L/L_T)$. The
three widths in the ladder set make the transfer length's width
dependence directly measurable.

\subsection{Van der Pauw (VDP)}

A symmetric cloverleaf gives $R_{sh}$ directly, with no contact-geometry
correction, from the implicit relation
\begin{equation}
  \exp\!\left(-\pi\,\frac{R_A}{R_{sh}}\right) \;+\;
  \exp\!\left(-\pi\,\frac{R_B}{R_{sh}}\right) \;=\; 1,
  \label{eq:vdp}
\end{equation}
where $R_A$ and $R_B$ are transverse four-point resistances measured
at 90$^\circ$ rotation. For $R_A \approx R_B \equiv R$, this collapses to
$R_{sh} = (\pi / \ln 2)\,R$. The four cloverleaves cross-check the TLM
slope and surface any width-dependent edge effects.

\subsection{Daisy-chain bond yield}

DCL6 and DCL12 put 6 and 12 LEDs in series through the K$_R$ cathode. One
I-V sweep across the endpoints (\texttt{DCL$n$\_IN}/\texttt{OUT}) exercises
$2N$ bonds per chain. A bad bond shows up as a discontinuity in $I(V)$
or a chain $V_F$ that overshoots $N \cdot V_{F,\text{nominal}}$. For
WL-SFCC red at 10~mA, $V_{F,R} \approx 2.05$~V \cite{wurth_wlsfcc}, so the
DCL12 threshold is roughly 25~V at 10~mA. The K$_G$ and K$_B$ cathodes of
every chain LED route to isolated probe pads, so a failure can be
localized to a specific die and a specific color rather than blamed on
the whole chain.

\section{Tooling and Workflow}

\textbf{Software.} KiCad 9.0.8. The \texttt{.kicad\_pcb} is written
programmatically by a Python script
(\texttt{tools/generate\_pcb\_text.py}) rather than placed through the
GUI. Fab outputs come out of \texttt{kicad-cli}: gerbers and drill
(required for Eurocircuits, optional for Aisler since Aisler reads the
native \texttt{.kicad\_pcb}), pick-and-place CSV, top and bottom review
PDFs, a 3D STEP model, and the fab-neutral BOM
(\texttt{tools/gen\_fab\_bom.py}) which parses identically at Aisler
Beagle and Eurocircuits.

\textbf{Fabrication.} Either Aisler or Eurocircuits can produce the board
on a 2-layer FR-4 1.55~mm pool with ENIG. The fab solders the 4 NTCs,
the reference resistor, and the 64 header pins (cut from two
1$\times$40 strips). The 26 WL-SFCC LED footprints are marked \emph{do
not populate} in the BOM and called out explicitly in the order notes,
since the LED bonding is the research subject and has to happen in the
TU~Delft cleanroom under controlled paste, reflow, and die-bonder
conditions. Three boards delivered, fully assembled apart from the LEDs,
costs roughly \euro115--125 at Aisler or \euro135--160 at Eurocircuits.

\textbf{Bonding.} LED placement runs on the Tresky T-3000-PRO. Its
95~$\times$~95~mm work envelope fits the 93~$\times$~93~mm board with 1~mm
of margin per side. Solder paste goes down through a 100~$\mu$m laser-cut
stencil keyed to the WL-SFCC pad layout (four corner pads, each
0.4~$\times$~0.4~mm at 0.8~mm pitch \cite{wurth_wlsfcc}). Reflow follows
IPC J-STD-020E with a peak between 245 and 260~$^\circ$C, the exact
profile depending on the paste vendor's datasheet.

\textbf{Measurement.} Keithley 2450 SourceMeter for V$_F$-TSP and DC I-V.
Solartron 1260A frequency response analyzer with a 1296A dielectric
interface for EIS. HP4156 parameter analyzer for the TLM and VDP fits.
T-type thermocouples soldered to the four \texttt{TC$n$} gold lands log
in-situ reflow temperature, which is useful when a paste lot starts
behaving oddly. Probe access is either through 1.27~mm Kelvin tips on the
\texttt{PP\_*} pads or through ribbon-cable breakouts on the 32-pin
headers.

\section{Solder-Paste Comparison}

The motivating experiment is a comparison of four commercial solder pastes
on identical hardware: same LED part, same substrate, same reflow profile
(within vendor spec), same characterization protocol. The four candidates:
\begin{enumerate}
  \item \textbf{SAC305} (Sn 96.5\% / Ag 3.0\% / Cu 0.5\%), the industry baseline.
  \item \textbf{SAC0307}, a low-silver variant. Cheaper, slightly worse on
        thermal cycling, similar V$_F$ drop.
  \item \textbf{Au-Sn eutectic}, the canonical micro-LED flip-chip
        bonding alloy, with reported low post-bond leakage current and
        high shear strength but a strong sensitivity to bonding
        temperature \cite{yin2024ausn}.
  \item \textbf{Polymer-reinforced solder paste}, an impact-tolerant
        chemistry developed for laser-assisted mass transfer that
        preserves solder-joint reliability while absorbing chip-drop
        energy \cite{ji2023polymer}.
\end{enumerate}
For each paste, a three-board batch (1 trial + 1 real + 1 spare, matching
Aisler's bundle size and convenient at Eurocircuits too). Per board,
measure: chain yield ($N_{ok}/N$ across
both DCL6 and DCL12), per-LED V$_F$ at 10~mA, $R_{th,bond}$ from the
V$_F$-TSP transient, $R_s$, $R_p$, and $C_j$ from a fit of EIS data to
(\ref{eq:eis}), and $\rho_c$ from a fit of TLM data to (\ref{eq:tlm}).
Cross-board variance is the headline number, since it captures how wide
each paste's process window actually is.

The same hardware supports a few other experiments. The 6$\times$6 DoE
bond-pad array (plain, minis, rounded variants) quantifies capillary
self-alignment yield against pad geometry. The four TC pads let the
reflow profile be measured and iterated against the paste datasheet. The
daisy chains, driven with a programmable current source, run accelerated
thermomechanical fatigue on a known set of bonds.

\section{Conclusion}

This v2 board puts four characterization methods (V$_F$-TSP, EIS, TLM,
VDP) and a daisy-chain go/no-go test on a single 93~$\times$~93~mm
substrate, so one cleanroom session produces a self-consistent measurement
of bond resistance, contact resistivity, sheet resistance, and thermal
resistance from the same hardware. The board is fab-ready on the
standard pool at both Aisler and Eurocircuits with ENIG plating, with the
design rules cleared at twice the published manufacturing minima. The
schematic, BOM, and tool chain are committed alongside this report so
anyone with KiCad and an account at either fab can re-fab a copy. Part 2 will report the first measurement
results from the four-paste comparison once the boards land and the
cleanroom session is done.
